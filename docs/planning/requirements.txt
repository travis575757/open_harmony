### 1. Core Algorithm (Native Rust Engine)

The core engine must be completely decoupled from any UI or file format, operating on a standardized internal data structure (e.g., a grid of `Note` events linked to `Voice` tracks).

#### 1.1 Data Ingestion & Automatic Voice Separation

When raw MIDI data is provided instead of strictly structured MusicXML, the engine must automatically parse and separate the notes into distinct contrapuntal voices (up to 4).

* **Quantization & Alignment:** Snap raw MIDI ticks to musical grid values (quarter notes, eighth notes) based on the provided time signature and tempo map, calculating the exact duration of each event.
* **Pitch-Clustering (Statistical Separation):** Use K-means clustering or running pitch-range averages to sort simultaneous or overlapping notes into distinct voice buckets (Soprano, Alto, Tenor, Bass).
* **Polyphonic De-interleaving:** When voices cross or share a staff, analyze the horizontal melodic trajectory (step inertia) to keep lines contiguous rather than just strictly assigning the highest note to the Soprano.

#### 1.2 Rule Engine & Configuration Matrix

The engine must use a modular trait-based system where individual "Rules" can be toggled on/off to create Presets.

* **Fuxian Species Presets (2-Voice):**
* *First Species (1:1):* Enforce only perfect/imperfect consonances. Flag any parallel 5ths/8ves, direct perfects, or melodic leaps larger than an octave/dissonant leaps.
* *Second Species (2:1):* Allow weak-beat dissonances *only* if approached and left by step (passing tones).
* *Third Species (4:1):* Enable rules for dissonant neighbor tones, double neighbors, and the *nota cambiata* idiom.
* *Fourth Species (Syncopation):* Detect tied notes across barlines. Enforce the preparation -> suspension -> downward step resolution syntax (e.g., 7-6, 4-3).
* *Fifth Species (Florid):* Allow mixed rhythms and eighth-note weak-beat subdivisions.


* **General Voice Leading (Common Practice SATB 18th Century):**
* *Spacing Rules:* Flag adjacent upper voices > octave, or Tenor/Bass > 12th.
* *Tendency Tones:* Flag unresolved leading tones () or chordal sevenths that do not resolve downward by step.
* *Doubling Rules:* Flag doubled leading tones, or incorrect doubling in 6/4 chords.



#### 1.3 Error Localization & Reporting

The engine must not just return a boolean "pass/fail". It must return structured error objects containing:

* **Rule Violated:** (e.g., "Parallel 5ths", "Unresolved Leading Tone").
* **Location:** The exact measure, beat, and tick.
* **Involved Entities:** The specific `voice_index` and `note_ids` that caused the infraction (e.g., the exact Soprano and Bass notes creating the parallel 5th).

#### 1.4 Harmonic & Melodic Analysis (Points of Interest)

* **Vertical Sonority:** Calculate the chord quality, root, and inversion (e.g., 6/3, 6/4) for every vertical slice.
* **Roman Numeral Analysis:** If a key signature is provided, output the functional Roman numerals.
* **Non-Chord Tone Tagging:** Label detected passing tones, neighbor tones, and suspensions based on their melodic approach/departure.
